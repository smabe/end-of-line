# Tron (1982) — Trivia Facts

- Director Steven Lisberger conceived the film after seeing Atari's Pong in 1976, making it a direct love letter to early arcade culture.
- The CGI sequences were produced by four separate effects houses — MAGI/Synthavision, Information International Inc., Robert Abel & Associates, and Digital Effects — none of which had ever collaborated before.
- The computers used for visual effects ran on just 2 MB of RAM and 330 MB of storage; each frame of CGI took 1–5 hours to render.
- Tron was disqualified from the 1983 Academy Award for Best Visual Effects because the Academy ruled that using computers was "cheating."
- French comic-book artist Jean "Moebius" Giraud designed the costumes and sets; industrial designer Syd Mead created the vehicle designs including the Light Cycles.
- Disney's traditional animators refused to work on the project, fearing that computer graphics would make their craft obsolete.
- A pre-release screening for Disney stock analysts caused the company's share price to drop $2.50 the following day.
- The film was moved from its planned Christmas 1982 release to July 9, 1982 to avoid competition with Steven Spielberg's E.T., and ended up competing with Don Bluth's The Secret of NIMH instead.
